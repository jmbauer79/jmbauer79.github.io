Delirium Village

A night in Brussels with Jase, tucked into the glow and bustle of Delirium Village, tasting rich Belgian beers in one of the city’s most famous beer destinations.

November 2025 · Brussels, Belgium

Some places announce themselves before you even walk inside. Delirium Village is one of those places — bright, busy, slightly chaotic, and full of the feeling that everyone has come looking for a good story, a good beer, or both.

Jase and I went at night, which felt exactly right. The lights, signs, narrow streets, and hum of people gave the whole place a festive energy. It was touristy, certainly, but in the best possible way: lively, memorable, and completely committed to its own sense of fun.

Belgian beer has a way of making an evening feel substantial. These are not casual, forgettable beers. They are rich, layered, sometimes surprisingly strong, and often served with a sense of ritual. At Delirium, the sheer variety becomes part of the experience.

Delirium Village felt like Brussels turning the volume up: bright, lively, indulgent, and impossible not to enjoy.

What made the night memorable was not just the beer, though that certainly helped. It was the combination of place and company — being in Brussels with Jase, surrounded by travelers and locals, trying something deeply tied to Belgian culture, and letting the evening unfold without needing it to be too polished or planned.

Travel often becomes vivid in these little nighttime scenes: a glowing street, a shared table, a drink you would not normally order at home, and the pleasure of realizing that the day has turned into exactly the kind of memory you hoped the trip would create.
