San Pedro de Atacama

Desert light, salt lagoons, impossible colors, wide skies, and the strange magic of floating effortlessly in the Atacama.

November 2024 · San Pedro de Atacama, Chile

San Pedro de Atacama felt like a place made out of contrasts: dry earth and bright water, silence and wind, empty horizons and landscapes so vivid they almost seemed unreal. The desert was vast, but never blank. Everywhere we went, there was texture — salt, rock, sky, heat, color, and space.

One of the most memorable experiences was floating in the salty desert water. The buoyancy was immediate and almost comical, as if the rules of the body had changed. Instead of swimming, I simply leaned back and let the water hold me.

The water was a beautiful shade of emerald blue, made even more striking by the surrounding desert. It was one of those travel moments that felt both playful and surreal: my feet in the foreground, the salt-rich lagoon holding me up, the sky above, and the dry Atacama landscape stretching out beyond the water.

The Atacama made the impossible feel casual: floating in blue desert water beneath a sky that seemed to go on forever.

What made the scene so memorable was the combination of opposites. Water in the desert. Weightlessness in a place defined by hard ground. Brilliant color in a landscape that could otherwise seem spare and lunar. The whole experience felt like stepping briefly into another planet.

San Pedro itself became the base for a wider encounter with the desert: salt flats, lagoons, flamingos, volcanic landscapes, night skies, and roads that seemed to run straight into the horizon. It was the kind of place where each outing felt distinct, but together they formed one larger impression of awe.
